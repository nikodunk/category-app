Who this review is for

This review is for consumers looking to find which ESCO is the best and the greenest. For information on solar panels, community solar, and other green options that have nothing to do with ESCOs, check out our section The competition.


Who should get this


Renters If you rent and you want the fastest, easiest way to use green energy, then ESCOs are for you. If you're looking for other options, check out our competition section. It’s much easier and quicker than installing hardware yourself, doesn’t require any cutting of red tape, and when you move, you can simply move your virtual contract on to your next apartment. For renters who don’t have time to consider other options, ESCOs are the way to go.


People who move a lot Even if you don’t rent, you may move a lot. In this case, there’s something to be said against being tied down by the sunk cost of solar hardware. ESCOs are much easier and quicker than installing hardware yourself, doesn’t require any regulation or red tape, and when you move (as renters sometimes often have to), you can simply move your virtual contract on to your next apartment.


People with shaded roofs or roofs facing the wrong way As we know, solar works best and in some cases only makes financial sense if your rooftop is facing the right way and isn’t shaded by any trees. In this case, solar can also make sense.


Customers looking to save money In some cases, switching to an ESCO can save you money. This is rarely the case, but in some states under some circumstances can be a thing. It is rare that ESCOs will actually save you money, as they are basically selling you the same commodity that the utility sells you anyway, but adding in cost for marketing and the renewable energy certificates (RECs). Check out a cool company called PowertoChoose for the cheapest rates in your area, though we find the site a little hard to use and the sourcing unclear.

The beginner's guide to ESCOs

TL;DR
ESCOs help you get a specific energy mix into your sockets, as opposed to your utility’s default mix. You pay through your normal energy bill.

ESCO stands for Energy Service Company. In states that are deregulated, energy is split up into supply and delivery. Utilities handle delivery, and also offer a default supply if you don’t tell them otherwise. ESCOs can handle over the supply part if you actively switch. You can not switch your delivery part. This has not been deregulated anywhere (and this guarantees a minimum of reliability in the electrical lines, etc).

When you sign up for an ESCO, ESCOs will purchase power credits from generation companies for the amount of energy you use every month. ESCOs do not generate their own electricity, but buy it from existing generation companies. You can not buy power from a generation company yourself as a single consumer. The ESCO will then bill you for this energy. That’s why on your bill, you always have two figures for supply and delivery. One is the ESCO, and one is the utility. Again, if you never sign up for an ESCO, the utility will just default to a mix of power as they see fit. This is what it’ll look like if everything’s working:

According to a Con Ed representative contacted by PolicyGenius, Con Ed has an obligation to get the best prices that they can, but ESCOs can provide certain deals that can make their prices lower than Con Ed’s.

Not all states are deregulated (as in, have split up supply and delivery), so in some states you can’t sign up for an ESCO and you still have to buy whichever energy your utility decides to buy. As of writing, New York’s Con Edison PowerYourWay website lists 72 different ESCOs that provide electricity, gas, or both.

Community Solar also known as shared solar. This is a great resource to directly finance more sustainable power. You develop a remote solar project with your neighbours, either for better bargaining power or because you can’t install solar locally on your rooftop. New York is leading a big push into community solar now, but it is still developing. The added organisational effort and longer timeline of these projects led us to the conclusion that ESCOs are a better solution for your average city person. If you have time and want to join a community – go for it! The best info and option to sign up for the wait list now and get on a list for NYC is again CleanChoice.
